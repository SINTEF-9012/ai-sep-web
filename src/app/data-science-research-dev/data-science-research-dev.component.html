Data Science Process

Data science is an interdisciplinary field focused on extracting knowledge from data sets, which are typically large (see big data), and applying the knowledge and actionable insights from data to solve problems in a wide range of application domains. The field encompasses preparing data for analysis, formulating data science problems, analyzing data, developing data-driven solutions, and presenting findings to inform high-level decisions in a broad range of application domains.

Key concepts

Data science process workflow

For smaller data science projects, e.g., experiments or one-man teams, rather than using a more well-defined data science framework, a starting point may be to follow a typical data science process workflow as depicted below. This workflow is used in the Harvard Business School introductory course on data science CS109 and represents a typical data science process. The lecture material for the course are available on GitHub.

The process consists of five steps:

  1. Ask an interesting questions: The first thing you have to do before you solve a problem is to define exactly what it is. You begin by asking questions to find the problem. You need to be able to translate data questions into something actionable. Things to consider in this step are:
    • What is the scientific goal?
    • What would you do if you had all the data?
    • What do you want to predictor estimate?
    • Is the problem suited for AI or Machine Learning (ML)? The "ML Project Checklist" may be used to answer that question.

  2. Get the data: Once you know what problem to solve, it is time to collect the right (raw) data to give you the insights needed to turn the problem around with a solution. Things to consider in this step are:
    • How where the data sampled?
    • Which data are relevant?
    • Are there privacy issues?

  3. Explore the data: A significant time of the data science process is spent spent on finding, cleaning and organizing data. Things to consider in this step are:
    • Plot the data.
    • Are there any anomolies?
    • Are there patterns?
    • Is the data unbiased?
    • Tools to consider in this steps are:
      • Jupyter notebooks
      • Pandas
      • Numpy
      • Matplotlib

  4. Model the data: Apply statistical modeling principles and machine learning to provide the abilÂ­ity to automatically create accurate predictive models about the future. Things to consider in this step are:
    • Build a model.
    • Fit the model.
    • Validate the model.
    • Tools to consider in this step are:
      • Jupyter notebooks
      • scikit-learn
      • Pandas
      • Numpy

  5. Communicate the results: Your result should make sense to other team members. One way to better communicate is by using visuals. Things to consider in this step are:
    • What did we learn?
    • Do the results make sense?
    • Can we tell a story?
    • Tools to consider in this step are:
      • Jupyter notebooks
      • Matplotlib
      • PowerPoint

Pros & cons

Pros Cons
  • Fits data science life cycle: Data science work needs a looser structure than deployment and engineering work.
  • Fits research backgrounds: Many data scientists come from research backgrounds and will find this approach natural.
  • Ad hoc data science: The initial data science phase could fall victim to the shortcomings of ad hoc processes.

Tool support

  • Jupyter notebooks for running experiments.
  • GitHub for code versioning and repositories.
  • NeptuneAI for tracking different experiments and is easily coupled with other tools and frameworks.
  • MLFlow an open source platform for the machine learning lifecycle.
