Why we see a need for an agile framework

Data science is an interdisciplinary field that uses scientific methods, processes, algorithms and systems to extract knowledge and insights from structured and unstructured data [Dahrm V, 2003]. It is related to data mining, machine learning (ML) and big data, and can be applied to data across a broad range of application domains.

The goal of this website is to provide a state-of-the-art (SOTA) overview of three areas within data science:

Explainable AI (XAI)
Explainable AI (XAI) is artificial intelligence (AI) in which the results of the solution can be understood by humans. As more and more AI solutions are being deployed, it is becoming increasingly important that we are able to explain the behaviour of the AI solutions so that we can trust that the predictions, recommendations and decisions they make are safe and non-discriminatory. The European Commission is currently proposing rules and actions for trust in high-risk AI systems. Such systems must be subject to strict obligations before being offered in the market.

This section of the website provides an overview of methods and tools for XAI based on the paper "Explainable AI: A Review of Machine Learning Interpretability Methods" by Linardatos et al. The paper gives a comprehensive overview of XAI resources and classifies them according to local vs global explainability, data types, purpose of interpretability, and model specific vs model agnostic applicability.
Agile AI Frameworks
Agile AI Frameworks are lightweight methodology frameworks suited to data science projects. In contrast to agile software development frameworks such as Scrum that focuses on incremental delivery of software products, AI frameworks have been adapted to support the data science process where the focus is on delivering actionable insights from data.

This section of the website provides an overview and description of the most popular agile AI frameworks, and a comparison table that gives you some guidelines for selecting the one that best fits your data science project. As a starting point we have used the paper "Identifying the most Common Frameworks Data Science Teams Use to Structure and Coordinate their Projects" by Saltz et al. The paper presents the results of a study of 109 professionals regarding the frameworks they use for data science projects. According to the study, CRISP-DM (CRoss Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) and Scrum are the two most popular frameworks in use.
Graph Data Science
Graph Data Science
Graphs used to represent connected data are gaining popularity in the area of machine learning and tools for graph analytics and graph-native machine learning are starting to emerge. A graph data structure has two basic elements: nodes and edges. Nodes represents entities in the data, while edges represents relationships between entities. The paper "On The Role of Knowledge Graphs in Explainable AI" by F. Leccue reviews explainable AI (XAI) and presents limitations and opportunities for Knowledge Graphs and their underlying technologies within different fields of AI, amongst others Machine Learning.

This section of the website provides an overview of graph analytics and machine learning tools available for two different types of graph data models: RDF Graphs and Property Graphs. Additionaly, it goes further into the research challenges and opportunities for Knowledge Graphs.
