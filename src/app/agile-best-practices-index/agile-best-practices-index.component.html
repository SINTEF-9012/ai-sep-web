Why agile for AI projects?

Agile software development was popularized by the Agile Manifesto as a backlash to the heavyweight waterfall-like methodologies. Agile prescribes a lightweight and more flexible approach to delivering software through iterations and increments.

While agile software frameworks such as Scrum have become the de facto way of developing and delivering software today, these frameworks do not necessarily translate well when applied to data science projects. There is a fundamental difference between delivering software products and delivering actionable insights from data as project results.

However, many agile principles still applies and are advantageous for data science projects:

  • Evolving requirements: Business owners and data scientists agree on high-level requirements early and and keep reviewing them as the project grows.
  • Increments and iterations: Data scientists produces evolving models and releases that are very user-focused. Insight into data comes from multiple iterations, not the first attempt.
  • Customer involvement: Customer gains a stronge sense of ownership being directly involved with the development team.

Overview of agile AI frameworks

To support data science projects, new agile frameworks have emerged and some existing agile frameworks have been adjusted accordingly. Today a few agile AI frameworks exists. According to a study presented in the paper "Identifying the most Common Frameworks Data Science Teams Use to Structure and Coordinate their Projects" by Saltz et al., CRISP-DM (CRoss Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) and Scrum are the two most popular frameworks in use. Surprisingly, "My Own" framework scored as the fourth in popularity.
